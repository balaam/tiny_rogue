1 - Dungeon floor

These tiles can be used in a variety of ways. The easiest way is to use these two patterns:

First fill the room with tile 1, then add the patterns.

-Don’t feel like you have to fill entire rooms with these patterns; breathing space is good.

-Having the patterns obscured in different ways by walls helps hide the repetition. Hugging the walls with details also looks good!

Just using these two patterns is enough, but with some experimentation you can create a bunch of different combinations. Here are some alternatives:

Tile 4 can be used to fill areas outside rooms.

2 - Dungeon floor autotile patches

Three autotile patches in the same layout. First square of nine are sides, corners & centre. Second four are inside corners. The first patch also has 4 alternate centre tiles.

These tiles cover every possible configuration as long as the area covered remains at least 2x2 tiles.

6 - Doorways

Doors work with or without the archway. Similarly, the archway works well without the door.

You could try fading out the archway while the player (or something else) is moving under it.

7 - Spike trap (animated)

Frame 1 - spikes down.

Frame 2 - moving up. (transition)

Frame 3 - spikes up.

Frame 4 - moving down. (transition)

Down to up: 1, 2, 3.

Up to down: 3, 4, 1.

8 - Torch (animated)

Row 1 - For wall-mounted torch.

Row 2 - Pillar-mounted torch.

Somewhere between 80-100ms per frame is a nice speed for these sprites.

The pillar/wall torches are identical. If you want to save space in your tilemap, they fit a single row of 5 tiles. (you will have to manually get the correct offsets, however).
